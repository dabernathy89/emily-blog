<h3>Day 4</h3>
<h4>Sunday Roast at Roast</h4>
<p>We knew we wanted to book a fun place for Sunday roast, so when I read about <a href="https://www.roast-restaurant.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Roast</a> at Borough Market on a list of child-friendly options, I made a reservation. The restaurant is beautiful, and the food was good. It is also definitely child-friendly &#8211; they gave Carson a children&#8217;s menu with a coloring page and crayons and had high chairs readily available.</p>

<h4>The Shard</h4>
<p>Stop #2 on this Sunday was a visit the top of The Shard, the tallest building in the UK. Tickets are expensive &#8211; a standard ticket to the top runs about $33 USD per adult, but there are lots of discount sites that offer tickets. Children under 3 are free. We had avoided this stop on previous trips to London because of the price, but I&#8217;m glad we did it this time. The views from up there are pretty incredible.</p>

<h4>The Airbnb</h4>
<p>After Sunday roast and a view from the Shard, it was already time to head to South London to check into our Airbnb. Again, we used <a href="https://www.familytransfers.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Family Transfers</a> to get us and all of our things from our hotel to our Airbnb. You can read my thoughts on the transfer company in <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2019/11/travel-with-me-baby-london-part-one/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">part one</a> of this series. Our Airbnb was a beautifully-designed <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/17657876" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">flat in the Crystal Palace area</a>. We relocated from our centrally-located Bermondsey Street hotel for a few reasons; primarily to save some money but also to stay somewhere that was particularly kid-friendly and walking distance to parks. Daniel had planned to be working some hours during the upcoming week, so I needed easy access to activities for Carson.</p>

<h4>Borough Market</h4>
<p><a href="https://bm.webstarsltd.com/visiting-borough-market" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Borough Market</a> is a must-do in London. It is a large market full of good food, locally-made crafts and gifts, flowers, produce, and souvenirs, all from a wide variety of local vendors. We could have spent a lot more time here, but we limited it to just a couple hours of wandering and eating some lunch. Carson enjoyed some food and then took a nice nap in his stroller after we snagged a seat to eat our lunch. As you can tell below, we were really drawn to the cheese and baked goods vendors.</p>

<h4>Tate Modern</h4>
<p>On recommendation from Jon, one of our Airbnb hosts, we walked over to the Tate Modern to spend a few hours. Jon had talked about how child-friendly this museum was, and we would never have considered it for a toddler if he hadn&#8217;t. We are so glad we went! There are entire sections of the museum dedicated to children. Carson even enjoyed parts of the museum that weren&#8217;t geared toward children from his stroller.</p>

<h4>Tonkotsu Ramen Bankside</h4>
<p>Since it was cold and rainy, I had a serious ramen craving. If you&#8217;ve read some of my previous Travel with Me posts, you will know this is not a rare craving for me on a trip! We shared a bowl of ramen and some small bites before beginning the long walk back to London Bridge Station to catch our train back to our Airbnb in Crystal Palace.</p>
