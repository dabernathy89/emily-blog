This October we took the risk, packed up our bags, and made our way across the Atlantic Ocean to the UK &#8211;Â  with our 18-month-old. I have always wanted to travel with our children during all life stages, and while we have done a bit of domestic travel with Carson, this was our first international trip as a family of three. So today I&#8217;m sharing our trip with you including all of the toddler-friendly things we did while there. I hope it can be used as a guide for others planning trips to London with littles.</p>

Of course we had some hard moments on this trip. For example, Carson did NOT want to sit still during our nice Sunday roast and, on another day, he refused to eat his children&#8217;s afternoon tea that we ordered as a special treat for him. But we didn&#8217;t have any major tantrums at all, and his sleep (which I was worried about the most of all) was essentially unaffected by the time change.</p>
<p>London is one of the most baby-friendly places I&#8217;ve ever been to. There are changing tables in the women&#8217;s AND men&#8217;s restrooms everywhere, nearly all restaurants we went to had high chairs and kids menus, coffee shops often had toy nooks for kids, public transit nearly always had a spot to park the stroller, etc. It was a wonderful family trip, and I cannot recommend London enough as a place to take small children.</p>
<h2>The Trip</h2>
<p>Our trip ran from a Thursday through the following Saturday &#8211; two travel days, six days in London, and two days in the Cotswolds. I am going to break up our time in London into three posts and our time in the Cotswolds into a separate fourth post.</p>
<p>This was also our first time visiting Europe in the fall. London is stunning in October with the changing leaves, cool weather, and mild crowds. Of course, there was some rain, but we didn&#8217;t mind it for the most part and just made sure to dress accordingly.</p>

<h2>Lodging</h2>
<p>For our first two nights in London, we stayed at the <a href="https://www.southpointsuites.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">South Point Suites</a> on Bermondsey Street, which was recommended to me by a friend. It was a great spot for our first weekend. The rooms were set up very nicely with a little kitchenette, and the bedroom was separate from the living area, so we were able to put Carson in his own room for his 7:30pm bedtime. The hotel provided a pack &#8216;n play for Carson to sleep in. The best part, however, was the location of the hotel. Bermondsey Street is full of wonderful-looking restaurants, bakeries, and coffee shops. We especially enjoyed the coffee from The Watch House, which was just a few feet from our hotel. Unfortunately, we ran out of time to try many of the restaurants. The hotel is about a 10 minute walk from the London Bridge Station, a major Underground and railway station that provides transit to nearly anywhere you want to go.</p>

<h2>Transportation</h2>

We got <a href="https://oyster.tfl.gov.uk/oyster/entry.do" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Oyster cards</a> at the Customer Service counter in the London Bridge Station and filled them up as needed. All modes of transport offered designated spots for strollers, so we were mostly able to keep Carson in the stroller for our rides. Additionally, many stations in London offer elevators &#8211; there was only one instance when we had to wait a very long time in line to get on the elevator. We used the Citymapper app for our route planning, and you can filter for accessible routes in your search to eliminate stations without elevators. I&#8217;ll go into more detail about stroller-friendly public transportation in London in a future post.</p>

And for our rides to/from the airport and between lodging locations, we hired <a href="https://www.familytransfers.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Family Transfers</a>. We were happy for the most part with our Family Transfers experience, barring one thing &#8211; the car seats provided were never properly installed and didn&#8217;t feel safe. Otherwise Family Transfers provided great customer service and timely, safe transfers. It&#8217;s nice to book private transfer to and from the airport, especially when traveling with a small child with lots of luggage.</p>

<h3>Day 1</h3>
<h4>Travel Day</h4>
<p>On Thursday, October 17th, our flight left Austin around 5pm. We had a short layover in Houston and then we were off on our overnight flight to London!</p>
<h3>Day 2</h3>
<p>We landed in London about 11am local time, very sleepy but excited for the day ahead! We checked into our hotel, cleaned up, and settled in. Then we went out to walk Bermondsey Street for a bit, grab coffee, get our Oyster cards, and before we knew it, it was time to head to our dinner reservation in Convent Garden.</p>
<h4>Dishoom</h4>
<p>Dishoom is an Indian restaurant that was recommended to me by a friend who had lived in London before. I was so excited to try this place out because of her rave reviews. We loved the food and the environment, and we were really glad we had a reservation. There was a two hour wait already at 5pm! We ordered the house black daal, garlic naan, gunpowder potatoes, chicken ruby (favorite!), and chicken tikka roll. It was all delicious!</p>

<h4>Afternoon Tea at Sketch</h4>
<p>Sketch is an adorable place to get afternoon tea in London. It isn&#8217;t your classic high tea that you&#8217;d get at Kensington Palace or elsewhere in London, but it was a neat experience for someone who had already had classic afternoon tea.</p>
<p><a href="https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148.jpg"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-10707" src="https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-986x1024.jpg" alt="Travel With Me &amp; Baby | London - Part One | Traveling with Baby | EverydayAccountsBlog.com" width="986" height="1024" data-wp-pid="10707" srcset="https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-986x1024.jpg 986w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-289x300.jpg 289w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-768x798.jpg 768w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-640x665.jpg 640w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-800x831.jpg 800w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-606x630.jpg 606w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148-600x623.jpg 600w, https://images.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/IMG_20191019_160148.jpg 1971w" sizes="(max-width: 986px) 100vw, 986px" /></a><br />
Carson was a bit grumpy because we had to wake him up from his stroller nap, but he was overall well-behaved. We didn&#8217;t really realize how nice this place was going to be since it had been listed on several child-friendly afternoon tea lists I&#8217;d found online, but it was fine. We made the last-minute decision to order the children&#8217;s tea for Carson thinking he&#8217;d enjoy the little bites and snacks, but he ended up having very little of it. It did come with a cute stuffed bear we got to take home though&#8230;. We ended up needing to be those parents who handed their child a tablet and pacifier in a nice restaurant, but we wanted to enjoy the experience. You gotta do what you gotta do!</p>

<h4>Hamleys</h4>
<p>After tea, we made one more stop at Hamleys, a famous toy store just around the corner from Sketch. It was already decked out for Christmas, so we had fun wandering around, checking out the toy sections for Carson&#8217;s current favorite shows (mainly Peppa Pig, Paw Patrol, and Thomas the Engine), and getting some good photos. We also picked up a few adorable Christmas books to take home with us.</p>
