</a>We spent our final three nights in Ireland in the lovely town of Galway. This town took me by surprise, and I fell in love with it. It boasts all of the wonderful Irish character found in Dublin (live music, great food scene, bustling locals), yet it has a certain charm and quaintness similar to the tiny Irish villages we visited along the route of our <a href="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2016/04/travel-with-me-our-ireland-road-trip-day-1/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">road trip</a>. I cannot recommend enough that you make Galway a stop on your Ireland road trip. We stayed in Galway for three days, and we used one of those days to drive around the Connemara region (read about that road trip <a href="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2016/06/travel-with-me-an-ireland-road-trip-sky-road-kylemore-abbey/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">here</a>). In this post I&#8217;m sharing what we did during our two full days spent staying in Galway. We loved every minute of our time there, so I recommend you follow this itinerary on your trip if you are looking for a mix of food (and coffee!), culture, and adventure.</p>

<h3>Galway City Museum</h3>
<p>Right next to the Arch is the Galway City museum. We spent an enjoyable hour learning more about the city, but it wasn&#8217;t my favorite museum as there wasn&#8217;t too much to see and a lot of the artifacts were out for preservation or visiting other museums. The admission is free, so it&#8217;s worth checking out.</p>

<h3>Galway Cathedral</h3>
<p>After learning about the city&#8217;s history at the museum, we walked over to the Galway Cathedral, yet another gorgeous European church. It was built in 1965, and it is the youngest of Europe&#8217;s stone cathedrals.</p>

<h3>Coffeewerk + Press</h3>
<p>I know I&#8217;ve already highlighted a Galway coffee shop, but the second one we ran across was one of our favorites we&#8217;ve ever been to. Coffeewerk + Press is a cute three story coffee and design shop. The experience is unique &#8211; you walk in and order your coffee (from a great selection of pour overs and coffee beans), and as it&#8217;s being made, you can explore the design shop on the other floors. The barista brings you your coffee when it&#8217;s ready, and you are free to drink it in the shop while flipping through one of the gorgeous design books or magazines. We had a great time! We also loved the coffee so much that we went back for another cup right before driving to Dublin on our last day in Ireland.</p>

<h3>Salthill Beach</h3>
<p>Early in the morning on our second day, we stopped at Salthill Beach to enjoy the view and the quiet for a few minutes. This beach is right outside Galway city center.</p>

<h3>A Typical Irish Dinner</h3>
<p>When we returned back to Galway after our drive, we had to decide where to have our last Irish dinner since we were leaving Ireland the next day. We decided on a typical Irish pub where we had a selection of Irish dishes to share. We even tried out black pudding which is essentially blood sausage. It sounds disgusting, but I actually thought it tasted pretty good.</p>
