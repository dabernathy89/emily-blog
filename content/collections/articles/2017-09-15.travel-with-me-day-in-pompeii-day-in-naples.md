Today I&#8217;m sharing a guide to a day in Pompeii and a day in Naples. In my last Travel with Me post, I laid out our time on Italy&#8217;s <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2017/08/travel-with-me-italys-amalfi-coast-sorrento-positano/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Amalfi Coast</a>, where we made our home base in Sorrento and took day trips to Positano and Pompeii. <h2>Pompeii</h2>
<p>You&#8217;ve probably heard of Pompeii, at least in your history textbooks. It&#8217;s that ancient Roman city that was completely buried by volcanic ash when Mount Vesuvius  erupted in AD 79. And the reason you&#8217;ve probably heard of it is because it was discovered in 1748 almost completely preserved and frozen in time, nearly 17 centuries later. This discovery has allowed historians and archeologists to learn about early 1st century Roman life in incredible detail. The population of Pompeii at the time of its destruction is estimated to be 11,000, and the city boasted advanced technology for its time, including an intricate water system and bath houses heated by geothermal energy.</p>

<h2>Naples</h2>
<p>As I mentioned above, a day trip to Naples from Sorrento or Rome is totally doable. Naples is an interesting town. Daniel and I talk about it as the only place in Italy that we didn&#8217;t fall completely in love with, but I will say that we weren&#8217;t there for very long and didn&#8217;t give it much of a chance. Naples is the third largest city in Italy, and it has a different feel than the other Italian cities we have visited &#8211; it&#8217;s dirtier and grungier, at least in the parts we saw.</p>
