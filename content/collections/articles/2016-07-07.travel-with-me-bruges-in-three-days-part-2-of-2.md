<p>I did some research on the place to get the best Belgian waffles and stumbled across a cute tea room that served lunch and waffles. <h3>De Halve Maan Brewery</h3>
<p>De Halve Maan is a very old, well-known Belgian brewery based in Bruges. We booked tickets for an afternoon tour because we are planners who book everything in advance, and we were glad we did. The tours sell out quickly, and both the Flemish and English tour groups were fully booked when we arrived.</p>
<p>The tour was great &#8211; the guide took us throughout the whole brewery, which was perfectly set up for tours and for teaching about the brewing process.</p>

<h3><span class="irc_pt" dir="ltr">Groeninge Museum</span></h3>
<p>The Groeninge Museum is a well-known art museum in Bruges that houses many famous Flemish paintings. It has beautiful exhibits, and we enjoyed looking at the paintings and reading some of the history of the painters.</p>
<h3>Belgian Waffles (Part 2)</h3>
<p>Since it was our last night in Belgium, we opted for Belgian waffles for dinner. We had passed this Belgian waffle &#8220;food truck&#8221; several times during our stay, so we stopped by on our way back to the apartment. Once you place your order, you can watch them make your waffles right in front of you.</p>
