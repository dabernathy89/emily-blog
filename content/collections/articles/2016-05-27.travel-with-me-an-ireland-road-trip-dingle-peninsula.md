<h2>Dingle Peninsula</h2>
<p>The Dingle Peninsula, while still a popular place for tourism, seems to be lesser known than its neighbor to the south, the <a href="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2016/05/travel-with-me-an-ireland-road-trip-ring-of-kerry/" target="_blank">Ring of Kerry</a>.Â Since we drove the two routesÂ within a day of each other, we were able to easily compare the two. The weather was less rainy for our drive around Dingle Peninsula which allowed us to take many more pictures, but it was just as cold andÂ windy. I think Daniel and I both agree that we thought the Dingle Peninsula was the more scenic and generally more jaw-dropping of the two routes, but you need to drive them both and make your own decision!</p>

<h3>Conor Pass</h3>
<p>Conor Pass is one of the highest mountain passes in Ireland, so usually it is a prime spot for incredible views. Unfortunately, the fog took away most of the view for us, but it was still beautiful.</p>

<h3>Beehive Huts</h3>
<p>A collection of ancient homes called the Fahan Beehive Huts sit just west of the town of Dingle. Their year of origin is not officially known, but some historians think they date back to the 12th century when Norman invaders forced farmers out from more prosperous areas of Ireland. This is an easy stop off of Slea Head Drive &#8211; just park your car on the side of the road, pay a person inside of a little hut a small admission fee (cash only), and walk up the hill to the ancient homes. Once again, we were the only ones there, so being alone along with the crazy wind and fog added to the mystery of the ruins.</p>
