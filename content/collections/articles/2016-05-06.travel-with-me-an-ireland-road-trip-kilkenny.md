<h2>Kilkenny</h2>
<p>Kilkenny, Ireland is a really fun town. We didn&#8217;t run into many other tourists and enjoyed the slower pace from Dublin.</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6428" src="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-1024x768.jpg" alt="2016-01-22 11.05.20" width="1024" height="768" data-wp-pid="6428" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-1024x768.jpg 1024w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-300x225.jpg 300w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-768x576.jpg 768w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-800x600.jpg 800w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-600x450.jpg 600w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20-1200x900-cropped.jpg 1200w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.05.20.jpg 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" />After a walk around town and a delicious Irish breakfast, we headed to <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Kilkenny Castle</strong></span>.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25.jpg"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6427" src="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-1024x768.jpg" alt="2016-01-22 11.06.25" width="1024" height="768" data-wp-pid="6427" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-1024x768.jpg 1024w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-300x225.jpg 300w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-768x576.jpg 768w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-800x600.jpg 800w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25-600x450.jpg 600w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/2016-01-22-11.06.25.jpg 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a><br />
The castle was built in 1195 by the first Earl of Pembroke. In the 1300s, it was passed on to the powerful Butler family (from which one of the daughters married the grandfather of Anne Boleyn), and then the last member of the Butler family gave the castle to the town of Kilkenny in in 1965 for the huge sum of  £50. Yes, you read that right &#8211; just £50! The State then refurbished the castle and the grounds and opened it to the public. It is now open for visitors every day of the year except for Christmas Day and the day after Christmas. During the summer you may enter on a self-guided tour, but the rest of the year, entrance is by tour only. We got tickets to a morning tour. Unfortunately, I don&#8217;t have pictures of the beautiful rooms and paintings to share because photos were not allowed.</p>

Many original pieces of furniture and artwork are still in the house, but there are also many pieces that have been lost over the years.</p>

We then made our way to the <strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Smithwick&#8217;s Brewery</span></strong> for a tour. The tour is very interactive and media-driven, and we learned a lot about the brewing process. Until reading about this tour online, I had no idea that there was another famous Irish beer other than Guinness, and we liked Smithwick&#8217;s even more. It&#8217;s very creamy, flavorful, and much lighter than a pint of Guinness.</p>

Unfortunately, we were way too tired to climb up all those stairs, but I&#8217;ve read reviews online saying the view of Kilkenny is stunning!</p>
