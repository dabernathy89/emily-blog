We took the train from Nice to Antibes and then Antibes to Cannes. It is very easy to explore the French Riviera by train &#8211; the TER (Transport Express Régional) is the train that has the most stops along the coast, including Antibes and Cannes (and Monaco which we explored the next day!). Our transportation was covered under our Europe Rail Pass, but you can get a one-way ticket on that route for around $6.50. It was a wonderful day!</p>

<h2>Ladurée</h2>
<p>When we arrived in Cannes after a very short train ride from Antibes, we saw a Ladurée soon after we started walking around, so we just had to stop for a macaron snack. We had been to the location in Paris before, but we thought this one was just as tasty and just as beautiful on the inside!</p>

<h2>Petit Train de Cannes</h2>
<p>We didn&#8217;t have too much time to spend in Cannes, so we knew we wanted to do some sort of tour that took us to all the major spots and taught us about the city&#8217;s history. The <a href="http://www.cannes-petit-train.com/en/" target="_blank">Petit Train de Cannes</a> was the perfect way to do that!</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-7400" src="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-1024x768.jpg" alt="2016-02-03 14.43.23" width="1024" height="768" data-wp-pid="7400" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-1024x768.jpg 1024w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-300x225.jpg 300w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-768x576.jpg 768w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-800x600.jpg 800w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-840x630.jpg 840w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23-600x450.jpg 600w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/2016-02-03-14.43.23.jpg 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" />We chose to do the Big Tour, a &#8220;two-in-one&#8221; tour covering both the history of the city and the luxe side of the city (think movie stars, beaches, fancy hotels and casinos, etc). After paying about $12 each for the Big Tour ticket, we hopped on the little train and put on our headphones. Once the train started moving, the pre-recorded tour began. While I definitely prefer to have a live person talk during a tour, we thought the recording was good, and the tour gave a good overview of Cannes.</p>
<p>The first part of the tour focused on the coast, the fancy hotels, and stories of movie stars who stay in the fancy hotels.</p>
