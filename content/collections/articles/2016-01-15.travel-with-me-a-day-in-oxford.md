The train journey to Oxford was much longer than to Bath (about an hour and a half), but it was still very convenient and worth the savings in hotel prices.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ve heard about Oxford my whole life through the stories about C.S. Lewis and Tolkien and the influence that Christ Church College had on Harry Potter&#8217;s Great Hall, yet I still didn&#8217;t know what to expect from this university town. We ended up enjoying our visit very much! I have my good friend Jennifer to thank for all of the wonderful recommendations, many of which are mentioned below. She attended Oxford for her graduate degree, so she is a great source!</p>
<p>In the descriptions below, I have underlined the attractions, restaurants, and things to do that I suggest adding to your one-day itinerary in Oxford based on my experience.</p>
<p>In our usual fashion, we started the day off with a walking tour around the city to orient ourselves and help us narrow down the list of things we wanted to do in our short time. We did the two hour <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Footprints Free Walking Tour</strong></span> based on the glowing reviews we read on TripAdvisor. The tour took us around to the outside of the Bodlein Library, the University Church of St. Mary, and many of Oxford&#8217;s colleges including Christ Church, All Souls, Merton, and Magdalen. The guide told us many interesting facts about Oxford as both a city and a university as well as stories about many of Oxford&#8217;s notable students. We really enjoyed the tour overall, but unlike the <a href="http://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2016/01/travel-with-me-bath/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">free tour in Bath</a>, this one encourages a tip, so be prepared with cash if you feel the guide deserves it.</p>

<p>Over lunch, Daniel and I planned out what we would do during our remaining few hours in Oxford. We decided to go tour two of the colleges next: <strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Christ Church</span></strong> and <strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Magdalen</span></strong>. Both of these colleges charge an admission fee ranging from $7 to $10 depending on the season.</p>
<p>I didn&#8217;t get very many pictures at Magdalen College because it was pouring rain at the time, but I got several at Christ Church. We toured the Great Hall, the cathedral, and other common areas. It would be very cool to be a student there!</p>

The journey up the tower is neat itself &#8211; there is a little exhibit before you reach the highest point. <p>It really started to pour after our college tours, so we ran to find the coffee shop our friend recommended, the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>The Missing Bean</strong></span>.  It had great coffee, and a fun, student vibe.</p>
<p>As library and bookstore lovers, we knew we had to do one of the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>guided tours of Oxford&#8217;s Bodleian Library</strong></span>. They offer a variety of options, including the mini, standard, and extended tours, and they encourage you to book your tickets ahead of time to secure a spot on your tour of choice. The mini tour (advertised as half an hour) was the only one that fit our schedule, but it ended up being longer (about 45 minutes) and very informative. Unfortunately, pictures were not allowed in Duke Humfrey&#8217;s Library, a gorgeous, centuries-old reading room. However, you can see it in three of the Harry Potter films because it was used as the Hogwarts library (Sorcerer&#8217;s Stone, Chamber of Secrets, and Goblet of Fire). This part of the library houses many, many important works that the students are actually allowed to reference under the supervision of a librarian.</p>

<p>After the library, we were still in the mood for books, so we went across the street to Blackwell&#8217;s Bookshop to check out the Norrington Room, a giant cavern of books. It was so neat! So many books in one place!</p>

<p>And that was our day in Oxford! My only regrets are not attending Evensong at one of the college&#8217;s chapels and and not visiting The Kilns, the home of C.S.
