<p>Photos from the week, top left to bottom right:</p>
<p>(1) Even though I was fighting a terrible cold all week (so bad that I stayed home from work), it was bearable because I used the extra time at home to watch more Christmas movies, read books, and enjoy my cozy Christmas apartment. Daniel was sweet enough to walk across the street for a Shake Shack Christmas Cookie milkshake one evening for me to enjoy while we watched The Holiday. (Have you tried this shake yet? It&#8217;s delicious!)</p>
<p>(2) Despite being sick, I had in mind that I wanted to do a few Christmasy things around town before we left Austin for Christmas. One of those things was going to the Trail of Lights at Zilker Park. It&#8217;s fun to get out and do these community events, especially during this time in our lives that we are able to live so close to the action!</p>
<p>(3) Saturday was our first family Christmas gathering at my great aunt&#8217;s house in Garland. My immediate family all rode together in the mini van from Waco to Garland. In Garland we got to see our grandparents, aunt, cousins, and the newest addition to the family, my cousin&#8217;s baby Ryker (who is a top contender for cutest baby on the planet!). Anyway, we ate a wonderful brisket lunch, played games, did our Secret Santa exchange, and all wore Santa hats, which is how the above photo of me and one of my sisters relates to this description. ðŸ™‚</p>
<p>(4) Christmas Eve and Christmas Day at my parents&#8217; house was absolutely perfect. We had a wonderful time with the family. We sat around the fire place and watched hours of Christmas movies, read books (I finished <a href="http://amzn.to/2BOJIWp" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mr. Dickens and His Carol</a> and loved it), exchanged gifts, ate lots of snack foods (including my grandmother&#8217;s delightful homemade cheese ball), played games, and decorated Christmas cookies. I&#8217;m going to be looking back fondly on Christmas 2017 for years to come!</p>
