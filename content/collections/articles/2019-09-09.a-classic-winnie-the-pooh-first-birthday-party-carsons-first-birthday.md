<p>I had so much fun planning my baby boy&#8217;s first birthday party. Since Carson hadn&#8217;t really developed his own &#8220;favorites&#8221; in terms of party themes by the time he was one, I picked classic Winnie-the-Pooh, purely because I thought it would be adorable and fun to put together. It was surprisingly difficult to find classic Pooh decorations, but in the end, I was able to find just what I wanted to complete the party look.</p>
<p>First, I found the perfect invitations on Zazzle.com and sent them out about six weeks before the party. We were so excited to host our first party in our new home!<a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Screen-Shot-2019-09-08-at-1.19.19-PM2.png"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10507" src="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Screen-Shot-2019-09-08-at-1.19.19-PM2.png" alt="A Winnie the Pooh-Themed First Birthday Party_Carson's First Birthday_EverydayAccountsBlog.com" width="517" height="419" data-wp-pid="10507" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Screen-Shot-2019-09-08-at-1.19.19-PM2.png 517w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Screen-Shot-2019-09-08-at-1.19.19-PM2-300x243.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 517px) 100vw, 517px" /></a><br />
Once the invitations were out, I slowly started collecting classic Winnie-the-Pooh items as I found them. I also started looking for my favorite photos of Carson so that I could print and hang them up in the windows.</p>
