Because we did so much during our time in The Eternal City, I&#8217;m splitting this guide to Rome into two posts &#8211; <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2017/11/travel-with-me-roma-part-1/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">day one</a> and days two and three. <p>As mentioned in <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2017/11/travel-with-me-roma-part-1/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">part one</a> of my Rome posts, our time in Rome was very group tour ‚Äúheavy‚Äù. On days two and three, we did a City Wonders <a href="https://citywonders.com/vatican-tours" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tour of the Vatican</a>, a¬†<a href="https://www.eatingitalyfoodtours.com/rome/tours/twilight-trastevere/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Trastevere twilight food tour</a> with <a href="https://www.eatingitalyfoodtours.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Eating Italy</a>, and a <a href="https://www.walksofitaly.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Walks of Italy</a> tour of <a href="https://citywonders.com/rome-tours/rome-crypts-and-catacombs-tour" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Rome&#8217;s Crypts and Catacombs</a>. Read more details about all three in their respective sections below.</p>
<h2>The Vatican</h2>
<p>As I mentioned above, we decided to tour the Vatican through another City Wonders tour. We had the same tour guide we loved from our Rome in a Day tour <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2017/11/travel-with-me-roma-part-1/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the day before</a>. She did a fantastic job of showing us the highlights of the Vatican and giving us some really interesting information along the way. I&#8217;m sure many of you know this, but I didn&#8217;t before the tour: Vatican City is the smallest country in the world. Vatican City&#8217;s economy is primarily operated on postage stamp sales and tourism.</p>

<h2>Lunch at¬†La Prosciutteria Trastevere</h2>
<p>After touring the Vatican Museum, we wondered over to Trastevere, a wonderful, quaint area of Rome that is full of wonderful foodie finds. For lunch, we went to La Prosciutteria, which is the cutest little Tuscan eatery that uses only the freshest locally-sourced ingredients. They serve the best things to be found at a table: wine, bread, cheese, and charcuterie!</p>

<h2><strong>Twilight Trastevere¬†<span class="il">Food</span>¬†<span class="il">Tour</span></strong></h2>
<p>After taking a break at the hotel, we headed right on back to Trastevere for our very first food tour (ever!) &#8211; the <a href="https://www.eatingitalyfoodtours.com/rome/tours/twilight-trastevere/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Twilight Trastevere Food Tour</a> by Eating Italy. It&#8217;s hard for me to believe that this was our first food tour since we have taken so many over the past two years, but this tour specifically is what ignited our obsession for food tours. I truly believe that touring a city through its food and the stories of the people behind the food is one of the best ways to start to understand a culture. I won&#8217;t go through all of our stops on the tour because there were eight stops over four hours. We had a wonderful night getting to know new people both on the tour and at the family-owned businesses while consuming Roman street food, the best wine and cheese, pizza, homemade cookies and gelato, and other creative Roman food concoctions. We even tasted a Nero d&#8217;Avola wine in a wine cellar that is older than the Colosseum (see dark, fuzzy photo below). If you are going to Rome, please do yourself a favor and take this tour!</p>

<h2>Walks of Italy¬†<strong>Crypts, Bones &amp;¬†<span class="il">Catacombs</span>: Underground¬†<span class="il">Tour</span>¬†of¬†<span class="il">Rome</span></strong></h2>
<p>At the last-minute, we decided to add in yet another group tour to round out our experience in Rome. We had heard someone on our previous day&#8217;s tour of the Vatican Museum talk about the <a href="https://www.walksofitaly.com/rome-tours/rome-catacombs-tour" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Walks of Italy Crypts &amp; Catacombs Tour</a> and how amazing it was, so we decided to book our spots. It was a fabulous and really educational tour! I was a little concerned it would be too creepy for me, and while it definitely had a tiny spooky element, that was overshadowed by the interesting stories and history shared by our guide. One of the most fascinating stops was beneath the Basilica of San Clemente, where you can see literal layers of history (including a street from 1st century AD), going further back in time as you continue to descend down into the site &#8211; our guide kept calling this a historical lasagna which I think is a pretty accurate description! Photos were not allowed in any of the crypts or basilicas that we visited, so I have no photos to share here. This tour&#8217;s primary stops are the Capuchin Crypt (aka ‚ÄòThe Bone Chapel‚Äô) and Basilica of San Clemente.</p>
<h2>Spirito di Vino &#8211; Final Meal</h2>
<p>For our final evening in Rome (and our final evening for our entire 2.5 month Europe backpacking trip!), we decided to bid farewell to Europe by returning to the restaurant above the wine cellar we had been to the night before on our food tour. I knew I specifically wanted to have some more of the Nero d&#8217;Avola wine we had on the tour, but we also wanted to try some more food items on their menu. We each ordered our final plates of fresh pasta and a bottle of that wine to share. Then we finished off our meal with wonderful desserts and a great conversation with the owners of the restaurant. What made this final visit extra special was that they sent us off with a bottle of that wine to take home with us completely free! Definitely plan to go to dinner at Spirito di Vino while you are in Rome. We cannot recommend it enough!</p>

<h2>Time to Go Home!</h2>
<p>And, just like that, it was time to go back to good ol&#8217; Texas. I can&#8217;t fully explain to you the mixed feelings I had the next morning as we headed to the airport in the early morning hours. We had just finished¬†<a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/tag/abernathy-adventure/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">a¬† two and a half month tour of much of Europe</a>, a complete dream of a trip that I know we more than likely will never be able to do again. The freedom we had to explore and jump onto trains and learn about new cultures and meet new people without any other commitments was an experience that I will never, ever forget. At the same time, we were very excited to get back home, see our loved ones (and our very missed¬†<a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/category/lazy-days-with-banana/.com/category/lazy-days-with-banana/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fur baby</a>!), and settle back into a routine.</p>
