<p>Photos from the week, top left to bottom right:</p>
<p>(1) Using the KonMari Method from <a href="http://amzn.to/2CDZrUJ" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Life-Changing Magic of Tidying Up</a>, I did a clear out of my clothing in my drawers and closet. It&#8217;s amazing how, even though we have moved five times during the nearly seven years of our marriage, I still managed to fill up four large trash bags of donations and one large trash bag of trash. And that is just clothing (my clothing &#8211; I did not even touch my husband&#8217;s). I also used the author&#8217;s method of folding to put things back into my drawers, and I&#8217;ve never had such beautifully organized drawers before! I&#8217;d like to use her method to continue clearing out various items in my home before Baby Boy arrives.</p>
<p>(2) This picture shows where I was most of the week when I wasn&#8217;t sick in bed, spending time with Heather and Cody, or cleaning out my closet. I spent lots of time on the couch reading and planning, in yoga pants with no makeup, and with a furry little acrobat by my side. üôÇ I&#8217;m actually meal planning in this picture.</p>
<p>(3) After church and lunch on Sunday, we took Heather and Cody for a quick walk up Mt. Bonnell for a nice view of Lake Austin and the surrounding beautiful homes and hill country. We got this great photo of them while we were up there!</p>
<p>(4) Daniel received the board game <a href="http://amzn.to/2AHAYfn" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Castles of Burgundy</a> for Christmas this year, and he has claimed that it is now his favorite game. I&#8217;m not a huge board game fan, so I had been putting off playing it with him until this weekend. I ended up playing a couple rounds of the game with him, Heather, and Cody on Sunday. After getting the hang of a specific strategy, I had a great time playing! If you like board games similar to <a href="http://amzn.to/2CETUx8" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Settlers of Catan</a>, I think you will like this one.</p>

<li>Reading and loving Anne Bogel&#8217;s book <a href="http://amzn.to/2qCkk0W" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Reading People: How Seeing the World through the Lens of Personality Changes Everything</a>. I¬†honestly did not expect to love this book, but I ended up giving it five stars on <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/user/show/3071331-emily" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Goodreads</a>, which is a rare occurrence for me. I&#8217;m looking forward to diving more into the Enneagram and the five love languages. (P.S. I&#8217;m playing with the idea of writing about what books I read each month and my thoughts.
