</a>As we made our way south by train from <a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/2017/05/travel-with-me-cinque-terre-in-two-days/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cinque Terre</a> to Florence, we could not have been more excited to explore the capital of Tuscany. The Tuscany I had seen in movies and read about in books seemed to offer many of my favorite things: pasta, cheese, wine, culture, a slower life pace (la dolce vita!), etc. I was so looking forward to diving in! We spent three nights in Florence (including one day touring the countryside), and we were very happy with how we spent our time, so I wanted to share it as an ideal three-night itinerary for travelers with similar interests.</p>
<h2>First Evening</h2>
<h3><a href="https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/74682" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Airbnb</a> Check In</h3>
<p>Our train arrived in Florence in the early evening. Our <a href="https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/74682" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Airbnb</a> host had given detailed instructions for check in, and we just needed to pick up the keys from the host at the restaurant across the street from the apartment building. It was about a ten minute walk from the train station. This apartment location was perfect for what we wanted to see and do in Florence, as it was right in the historic district of the city. The space itself is very well-designed and comfortable. If you click over to the listing at Airbnb, you will see from the amazing reviews that we are not the only ones who enjoyed our stay!</p>

<h3>Strolling Down the Ponte Vecchio</h3>
<p>Once we dropped off our things, took photos of the apartment, and cleaned up, we headed out to see the city at night and find a bite to eat. The apartment was very close to the Ponte Vecchio, so we walked across the river on it. The bridge itself holds many jewelry shops &#8211; it was beautiful with the shops all lit up on the bridge at night!</p>
<p><a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427.jpg"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-9199" src="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-1024x576.jpg" alt="" width="1024" height="576" data-wp-pid="9199" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-1024x576.jpg 1024w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-300x169.jpg 300w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-768x432.jpg 768w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-800x450.jpg 800w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-1120x630.jpg 1120w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427-600x338.jpg 600w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/20160304_184427.jpg 2048w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a>Across the bridge, we were happy to see streets full of amazing looking restaurant options filled with all kinds of people &#8211; locals and tourists alike. We were in the Oltrarno area of Florence, and everything seemed so full of life! We walked along the streets, taking in the sights and sounds, and came across an interesting looking Italian deli. We ordered a couple sandwiches for dinner. The deli had a cute painting of the Ponte Vecchio on the ceiling (see photo below).</p>

<h2>Day 2</h2>
<h3>Morning Stroll</h3>
<p>We started our morning out by leisurely walking near the Ponte Vecchio on the other side of the river to catch the view of the bridge by day. We then headed to the meeting spot for our upcoming tour. Florence is such a beautiful city, day or night!</p>

<h3>Wine Tour</h3>
<p>We took the <a href="https://www.tuscan-wine-tours.com/super-chianti" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Super Chianti Tuscan Wine Tour by Grape Tours</a>, and it was amazing! The tour offered eight hours of bliss and all things Tuscan: rolling hills, cheeses, meats, bread &amp; olive oil, and, of course, wine! Our guide Matteo was very knowledgable and passionate about the wine, food, and people of Tuscany. He was able to teach us well and give us a glimpse of Tuscan culture. This tour is only available March through October, and our time here was at the very beginning of March. We were happy to catch the beginning of tour season.</p>
<p>We visited three wineries in the Chianti and Chianti Classico regions and had tastings at each one. At one of the wineries, we had cheese and bread and olive oil with our wine. We were able to tour two of the wineries. Additionally, we stopped at a local Italian butcher for lunch and stopped at another small village to explore.</p>

<h3>Uffizi Gallery</h3>
<p>We were exhausted at this point in the day, but we could not miss the opportunity to explore the Uffizi Gallery, a world-renowned art museum primarily showcasing artworks from the Renaissance period. Masterpieces created by Botticelli, Raphael, Michelangelo, and numerous others are showcased in a beautifully designed set of exhibits.  The museum is closed on Mondays, so keep that in mind, but it stays open until about 7pm the rest of the week. During the summer, they offer some extended hours until 10pm, so check the website before going. Not many museums in Europe are open that late, so it&#8217;s a good way to get your visit in even if you have a busy morning and afternoon already planned. Keep in mind that Uffizi is a large museum &#8211; we didn&#8217;t allow much time for our visit, so we weren&#8217;t able to see everything.</p>
