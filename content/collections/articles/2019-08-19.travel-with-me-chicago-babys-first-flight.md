<h2>The Trip</h2>
<p>Our time in Chicago during July 2018 was part family vacation and part web development conference for my husband Daniel. Since I was in the middle of maternity leave, it was the perfect time for Baby Carson and I to tag along. We were so excited but so nervous about our first big trip with Carson. We flew in on a Saturday, and we had through Tuesday to enjoy Chicago together as a family. Then Tuesday through Thursday Daniel had his conference while Carson and I hung out and took it easy. We flew home Friday.</p>
<h2>The Flight</h2>
<p>Again, I&#8217;ll be sharing more practical information based on what I learned flying with my three-month-old in a future post, but I had to document Carson&#8217;s first flight here! He did great and slept like a dream in his <a href="https://amzn.to/31HXgvx" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">DockaTot</a>. For a baby that didn&#8217;t nap very well at this age, we were pleased to have a flight filled with snoozes and smiles. Even the airport experience itself (checking in, getting through security, boarding the plane, etc.) went just fine.</p>

<h2>Transportation in the City</h2>
<p>One of my concerns when planning this trip was how we were going to get around with a baby.Â  In the recent past, we did take a lot of public transportation, but we could also easily call a Lyft or Uber which we were now unable to do since we would have needed a carseat. However, using the Chicago public transportation system was no problem for us at all. You will see below in Day 1 that we opted for a private transfer to and from the airport because we had a lot of luggage, but the rest of the time we either walked, rode the Metra rail system, or rode buses. I discuss some of this more below, but in the end, it was very easy to use public transit, even with a full diaper bag and an infant in a stroller.</p>

That being said, here is what we did each day during the trip.</p>

<h4>Monteverde</h4>
<p>Our morning walk ended at Monteverde, an Italian restaurant in the West Loop. We met my friend Suramya who lives in the city there for brunch. It was so good to see her and for her to meet Carson! Unfortunately, Carson left a surprise for her in the form of his first blowout while she held him. Thank goodness, she has a little niece and nephew and loves babies! <em>Of course</em> his first big blowout ever would be in a nice restaurant on my friend wearing a white shirt! Anyway, Monteverde was a great option for a nice brunch in the West Loop, even with a baby. There were several families with kids there, and they let us push the stroller next to the table, so it&#8217;s definitely family-friendly (at least for brunch). Definitely <a href="http://monteverdechicago.com/reservations/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">make a reservation</a> though &#8211; the place is popular!</p>
<p>After brunch, we walked back to the hotel for naps and showers. While it was much cooler in Chicago than at home in Austin at this time, we were still sweaty by the time we got back from our walk.</p>

<h4>On Tour Brewing</h4>
<p><a href="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294.jpg"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-10356" src="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-768x1024.jpg" alt="Travel With Me | Chicago | Traveling with Baby | EverydayAccountsBlog.com" width="768" height="1024" data-wp-pid="10356" srcset="https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-768x1024.jpg 768w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-225x300.jpg 225w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-640x853.jpg 640w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-800x1067.jpg 800w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-473x630.jpg 473w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294-600x800.jpg 600w, https://www.everydayaccountsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/IMG_7816-e1565981575294.jpg 1536w" sizes="(max-width: 768px) 100vw, 768px" /></a><br />
We then made our way to On Tour Brewing, but we took the long way! We took the Metra from lunch to the brewery, but before we got there, we went the opposite direction on the Metra for THIRTY MINUTES. We blame the fact that there are two Metra stops on the same line with a very similar name, but it was probably just &#8220;mom and dad of an infant brain&#8221;. We were glad that Carson chose this time to nap or else he probably would have gotten a bit impatient.</p>

<p>Once we arrived at the brewery, we enjoyed sitting in the air conditioning sipping a few samplers. The brewery was great and had lots of space &#8211; we recommend it!</p>
<h4>Forbidden Root Restaurant &amp; Brewery</h4>
<p>We ended this day with dinner at another brewery, Forbidden Root. The restaurant here was much nicer than we expected, but the food was wonderful. We shared a roasted chicken plate and a cheese plate for dinner. Yum!</p>

While they were hard, they were also sweet days because we spent lots of time playing and walking around the city together.</p>

<h3>Travel Home</h3>
<p>Our flight home was just as successful as our flight to Chicago, but we did arrive home very ready to be back.</p>
<p>I hope that this post gives you some inspiration for your next trip to Chicago, with or without a baby. Even though we packed in a lot less than we would without Carson in tow, we certainly did more than I thought we&#8217;d be able to manage with him. This was the first of several trips we have taken with our baby, and I look forward to sharing more travel guides and tips &amp; tricks in upcoming posts!</p>
